A Look at the Celtics Starting 5 This Season

Table of Contents

As the 2023-24 NBA season unfolds, the Boston Celtics emerge with a roster blending seasoned veterans and invigorating new talent, poised to dominate the hardwood. In this pivotal year, a few players stand out for their statistical achievements and leadership, tenacity, and ability to galvanize the team toward victory.

Below, we’ll spotlight the starting five of the Boston Celtics this season, dissecting their contributions and forecasting the impact they’re poised to make. From game-changing plays to inspiring moments of leadership both on and off the court, these athletes are the heart and soul of the Celtics’ quest for glory.

Jayson Tatum

With Jayson Tatum’s talent and prowess, the Celtics Spread Bets and other bets like the moneyline, totals, and parlay show promising wins for the bettors.

February of 2023 proved to be an outstanding month for Jayson Tatum. He participated in his fifth NBA All-Star game and was pivotal in leading the Celtics to achieve a remarkable 9-1 record throughout the month.

Throughout February last year, Tatum showcased exceptional performance, scoring an average of 27.2 points per game across 10 games played by Boston, which led to him being recognized for his outstanding efforts.

However, Tatum received the accolade of Eastern Conference Player of the Month last month, marking the fourth occasion of such recognition in his career. His impressive scoring average came from shooting 48 percent from the field and 35.1 percent from beyond the arc.

Tatum topped the Celtics’ scoring charts in half of their February games with notable performances, including scoring 41 points against the Brooklyn Nets, 35 against the Washington Wizards, and 34 against the Memphis Grizzlies.

Apart from a game where he posted 19 points in a victorious effort against the New York Knicks, Tatum consistently scored over 20 points in the month’s contests.

Jrue Holiday

The Boston Celtics were disappointed with their consecutive defeat at the hands of the Atlanta Hawks this Thursday night, especially following the disheartening game last Monday.

Yet, amidst the setbacks, there was a silver lining: guard Jrue Holiday returned to the starting lineup. Holiday has become an integral part of the Celtics this season, often called the team’s “glue guy,” underscoring the increasing importance of securing his place on the team for the future.

Raised in Los Angeles, Holiday was a passionate Lakers fan, looking up to legends like Magic Johnson and Kobe Bryant. His support for the team in purple and gold was unwavering as they clinched NBA titles during his youth and through his single season at UCLA in 2008-09.

Holiday, renowned as one of the premier guards proficient in offense and defense, has joined forces with the dynamic pair of Jayson Tatum and Jaylen Brown. This duo, eager to secure their inaugural championship, propelled the Celtics to the Finals in 2022 – their first appearance in 12 years, and to the Eastern Conference finals in the subsequent season.

Jaylen Brown

Boston Celtics All-NBA forward Jaylen Brown is exhibiting exceptional court performance at the beginning of the NBA’s 2023-24 season. Originating from Georgia, Brown has enhanced his decision-making while navigating defenders, improved his ball-handling skills, and evolved into a proficient distributor.

His synergy with Kristaps Porzingis’s recent addition has revealed fresh dimensions in his gameplay, enhancing his skill in balancing slow-paced movements with aggressive drives to the basket.

Brown’s game is becoming more refined, showing a notable improvement in his decision-making and ability to provide what the game demands instinctively.

Kristaps Porzingis

After his trade from the Dallas Mavericks in 2022, Kristaps Porzingis has successfully settled with the Celtics. Taking on a critical role for the top-performing team, he averages 19.5 points, 6.8 rebounds, and 1.9 blocks over 32 games.

Celtics coach Joe Mazzulla emphasized the importance of Porzingis’ ability to influence the game both inside the paint and from the perimeter. He elaborated on how this dual-threat presence necessitates the team to adapt to varied defensive strategies and match-up adjustments during a game.

Derrick White

Derrick White has undergone a remarkable transformation from an institution that has not produced any other NBA talents to being among the most effective two-way players in the league.

Starting his college basketball journey at the University of Colorado–Colorado Springs, a school with no other NBA alumni besides him, White has made significant strides.

Before the Boston Celtics’ commanding 134-101 win against the San Antonio Spurs on New Year’s Eve, his previous head coach, Gregg Popovich, acknowledged White’s evolution into an All-Star-level player in his seventh year.

Hailing from Parker, Colorado, he has quickly become one of the most influential two-way players in the league. According to dunksandthrees.com, he is among only four players to be ranked in the 92nd percentile or higher for estimated plus-minus on offense and defense. The others in this elite group include Kawhi Leonard, Shai Gilgeous-Alexander, and Joel Embiid.

Final Words

As the 2023-24 NBA season is underway, the Boston Celtics’ roster has again demonstrated its depth and talent, keeping the team at the forefront of championship contention. The performances of the top five season leaders have showcased individual brilliance and underscored the power of teamwork, resilience, and strategic play. These athletes have risen above challenges, set new records, and inspired their teammates and fans.
