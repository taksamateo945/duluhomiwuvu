Who is Whitney Cummings Husband? Find out more in this Article.

Whitney Cummings is a renowned comedian, TV personality, actress, podcaster, writer, producer, and director.

Her career took off with stand-up comedy, and she frequently appeared as a roundtable guest on E!’s late-night talk show, ‘Chelsea Lately.’

Whitney gained recognition as a roaster in Comedy Central Roasts, including those of Joan Rivers (2009), David Hasselhoff (2010), and Donald Trump (2011).

She has delivered several hour-long comedy specials, such as

  • TV Shows:
    • NBC’s “Whitney”

  • Recognitions:

Whitney ventured into writing and published her first book, ‘I’m Fine…And Other Lies,’ in 2017.

She entered the world of podcasting in November 2019 with ‘Good for You,’ where she has hosted various influential figures in the industry.

Whitney self-financed her fifth stand-up Netflix special titled ‘Whitney Cummings: Jokes,’ attracting a substantial following curious about her dating life.

Whitney Cummings Husband

Whitney, who has become more private about her love life, is currently in a serious relationship with veterinarian Alex Barnes.

The couple’s intriguing story began during the COVID-19 pandemic, thanks to an unfortunate incident involving one of Whitney’s foster dogs.

In an interview on ‘LIVE with Kelly and Ryan’ in August 2021, Whitney revealed that after the dog was accidentally injured, she desperately sought out a veterinarian.

Despite being turned away by several professionals due to the pandemic, Alex recognized her and offered his assistance.

Whitney mentioned that she was instantly drawn to Alex, who is younger than her.

After a few conversations and meetings, they officially started dating in July 2021.

During her ‘Touch Me Tour’ in October of the same year, Whitney surprised her audience by expressing her desire to be the one to propose to Alex, going against traditional norms.

She humorously mentioned her lack of success in choosing partners and relationships, emphasizing her urgency to take matters into her own hands. Whitney humorously stated that she would even buy her own engagement ring since the one she desired was beyond Alex’s budget.

Whitney also shared her thoughts on potentially starting a family with Alex, revealing her preference for first undergoing a 23+ Me test before considering having children.

She froze her eggs in 2015 due to fertility concerns caused by time constraints.

Cummings and Alex are in a joyful and promising stage of their relationship, and we wish them a future filled with love and success.

Whitney Cummings’ Family

Whitney Cummings, a 39-year-old woman, was born in Washington DC to parents from Texas.

Raised in a Roman Catholic household, Whitney experienced a challenging childhood due to her parent’s divorce when she was five.

She has an elder half-brother named Kevin and a sister named Ashley.

Describing her upbringing as “tumultuous,” she attributes this to her household’s “alcoholic” and “dysfunctional” dynamics.

As a result, her relationship with her immediate family seems distant.

However, Whitney holds a special place in her heart for her work family, and the people she has learned from and grown up with throughout her career.

She moved about a lot when she was younger and, at age 12, was living with her uncle and aunt in Virginia.

Later, in 2004, she received a magna cum laude degree from the University of Pennsylvania’s Annenberg School for Communication.

She graduated with a degree in communications, although her original goal had been to work as a journalist.

Thankfully, everything changed when Whitney began performing stand-up at the age of 22.

Whitney Cummings’ Ex-Fiance

Whitney Cummings entered a relationship with Miles Skinner, a Creative Director and Media Strategist whom she met online in early 2017.

After more than a year of dating, they became engaged in September 2018 when Miles proposed on Whitney’s birthday.

Celebrating the engagement, Whitney shared her excitement, emphasizing the importance of personal growth, intuition, and humility in finding love through a dating app.

Unfortunately, after approximately a year and a half of being engaged, Whitney and Miles decided to end their relationship in early 2020.

The split was amicable, with no animosity or drama involved.

In a February 2020 episode of her podcast ‘Good For You,’ Whitney clarified that she was no longer wearing an engagement ring and expressed that the decision to call off the engagement stemmed from her own readiness to plan a wedding.

The prospect of wedding preparations became overwhelming, particularly given her mother’s health struggles after a stroke.

Whitney felt that the timing and circumstances were not conducive to creating a joyful wedding experience, as she wanted the process to be enjoyable rather than a burden.

Whitney’s decision to end the engagement was primarily influenced by her desire to prioritize her mother’s well-being and ensure that her focus was on supporting her through the challenging times.

Planning a wedding felt emotionally difficult and was overshadowed by the sadness surrounding her mother’s health situation.

Whitney emphasized that the choice was not a result of any negative factors within the relationship but rather her personal circumstances and emotional state at the time.
